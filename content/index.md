### What You’ll Find Here

Stories that lean sideways.  
Fiction threaded with a little magic, a little grief, and a lot of truth dressed in strange clothes. Columns that walk the line between memory and observation, wonder and worry.

This is a quiet archive of the human weird—told with a raised eyebrow, a Southern drawl, and a belief that the smallest moments are often the most profound.

The short stories here aren’t fireworks. They’re more like strange postcards from a place just outside the map: a roadside diner that serves memory instead of pie, a bus that loops through choices instead of streets, a barista who knows your dreams before you do. Each one stands on its own, but if you read between the lines, they start whispering to each other. Something bigger, maybe. Or just something we’ve forgotten.

The columns are grounded in the real world—mostly. They’re about the quiet tension of everyday life, the weird things people say when they think no one’s listening, and the way a good sentence can still land like lightning. Some of them are funny. Some of them hit harder than expected. Most of them are both.

This isn’t a blog. It’s not really a portfolio either.  
It’s more like a memory palace for things that haven’t happened yet.

So come in, take a seat, read a little.  
If you leave with a feeling you can’t name, then it’s doing its job.
